PBL 1314 EKG
The P waves (green arrows) are much taller than normal in leads II and aVF, indicating right atrial abnormality (sometimes called right atrial enlargement).  The QRS voltage is low in all limb leads, and especially in lead I.  The QRS axis is rightward (~90o.)  Also sometimes seen is a qR in V1 and early transition from RV hypertrophy, but not seen here.  This EKG shows pulmonary disease pattern.
