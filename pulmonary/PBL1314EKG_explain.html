PBL 1314 EKG
The P waves (green arrows) are much taller and peaked than normal in leads II and aVF, indicating right atrial abnormality (sometimes called right atrial enlargement).  The QRS voltage is low in all limb leads, but especially in lead I, as shown by the enclosing red arrows which indicates the QRS axis is close to perpendicular to that lead, so rightward (~90o).  Also sometimes seen is a qR in V1 and early transition from RV hypertrophy, but not seen here.  This EKG shows pulmonary disease pattern.
