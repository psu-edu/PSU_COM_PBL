PBL CASE 1310:  Orientation to Spirometry, 5 year old boy
The upper graph shows two loops of the exhaled flow (vertical axis) versus the exhaled volume (horizontal axis).  The lower graph is the exhaled volume versus time from the same test, but rotated to make it clear that it is the same volume as shown in the flow-volume loop.  While these graogs show the same information, it is harder to detect subtle abnormalities when viewing volume versus time.  Therefore, it is customary to display the spirometry as flow-volume loops.  The inhaled phase is often not shown in the time plot.  The blue traces are before treatment, and the red trace after bronchodilator treatment in each graph.
