PBL CASE 1310:  Orientation to Spirometry, 5 year old boy
The upper left graph shows two loops of the exhaled flow (vertical axis) versus the exhaled volume (horizontal axis).  The data is derived from the volume versus time in one the right.  The inhaled phase (the bottom of the loops) is often not shown in the time plot.  Theblue traces are before treatment, and the red traces after bronchodilator treatment in each graph.  The volume versus time graph is rotated to show the relationship of volume versus time to volume versus flow.  While these graphs show the same information, it is harder to detect subtle abnormalities when viewing volume versus time.  Therefore, it is customary to display the spirometry as flow versus volume.
