Palmar Physical Exam

Visual inspection of the left hand reveals two separate abnormalities that are hard to distinguish.  Osler's nodes, left, are painful ("Ouch") slightly raised, red lesions, usually on the fingers and toes.  They are from immune complex deposition, in small vessels, and take weeks to resolve.  Janeway lesions, right, are painless ("Oh, Joy") red to purplish lesions, usually on the palms and soles.  They are caused by bacterial clusters that embolize and create microabscesses on small arterial walls.  They clear in about a week, or more quickly than Osler nodes.
