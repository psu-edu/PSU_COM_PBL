Rheumatic Aortic Stenosis

The view of the aortic valve looking down on the valve.  The pulmonary trunk (main pulmonary artery) is shown with an asterisk.  The aortic root is in the center of the image.  The right coronary artery is filleted open (by the pathologist) along its length (red arc).  The left main coronary artery is also filleted open along its length (blue arc).  The aortic valve leaflets show remarkably little calcification.  The aortic valve has 3 cusps but the commissures have fused (black arrows) from chronic rheumatic valvulitis.  The fusion greatly restricts the valve opening.
