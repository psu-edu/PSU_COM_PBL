CXR 1

The large left atrium creates a right side "double density."  The left atrium is outlined in red.  The large left atrium lifts the left mainstem bronchus and that splays the carina (yellow angles).  It is sometimes more dramatic than seen here.  The LV is dilated and the LV apex is displaced laterally (purple arrow).  Due to increased pulmonary congestion, there is "cephalization" of the lung parenchyma, with "nearly" as dense upper lung fields (red arrows) as the lower lung fields (blue arrows), so the normal decrease in parenchymal density toward the apices is lost.
